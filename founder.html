The Founder's Story

I have always believed that the most interesting place to be is where two distinct forces collide. My journey has taken me from a small village in Alaska to studios around the world, but my philosophy remains the same: real magic happens at the intersection.

I’m building this resource because ALS runs in my family. I’ve seen first-hand the need for quality of life improvements for people with mobility issues or those who are recovering from injury.

It became clear to me that AR and VR technology shouldn't just be for gaming or enterprise—it can be implemented in a way that tangibly improves people’s lives. Whether it's providing a digital escape, a new way to interact with the world, or a tool for rehabilitation, the potential is limitless.

In 2024, I founded Revivor Studios. The name is a union of "Survivor" and "Revive"—a testament to those simply surviving their conditions, and a promise to help revive their sense of wonder. We build VR and AR experiences specifically for people with challenging physical disabilities, limited mobility, or phobias.

My goal is to use my decades of technical expertise to build tools that don't just distract, but heal. We are expanding worlds for those whose physical space has become limited, proving that while the body may be confined, the human experience should remain boundless.
